Voluntary Community Service Project to Help the Disabled in the Community

During my Grade 11 and 12 in Thompson, Manitoba, I organized a voluntary community service project under the guidance of my Engineering-Design-Drafting (30S) teacher to assist a resilient, specially-abled man in our community. The goal was to design and implement a removable tray for his wheelchair to help him perform his job duties more efficiently.

Our team successfully drafted the tray using the resources available in our drafting class but encountered a significant challenge in securely attaching it. The point of attachment conflicted with the wheelchair's securement system, potentially causing safety issues during transport on ramps or in vehicles.

Although we couldn’t achieve the intended outcome, this experience taught me invaluable lessons about problem-solving, perseverance, and the importance of initiative over success or failure. This project inspired me to explore alternative solutions and reinforced my commitment to addressing unique challenges within my community.

I believe the long-term impact of this project lies in inspiring others to take on initiatives that promote compassion, support, and inclusivity. This effort has shaped me into a more empathetic and resilient individual and contributed positively to my community's growth.
